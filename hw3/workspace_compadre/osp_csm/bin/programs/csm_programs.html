An Introduction to Computer Simulation Methods
Programs

There is no substitute for a short demonstration program.  This java archive (jar file) contains ready-to-run Java programs from An Introduction to Computer Simulation Methods by H. Gould, J. Tobochnik and W. Christian (Prentice Hall 2007).

Double-clicking on a leaf-node in the Launcher's left-hand "tree" to run examples from the following chapters:

  • Chapter 1: Introduction
  • Chapter 2: Tools for Doing Simulations
  • Chapter 3: Simulating Particle Motion
  • Chapter 4: Oscillations
  • Chapter 5: Few Body Problems
  • Chapter 6: Chaotic Motion of Dynamical Systems
  • Chapter 7: Random Processes
  • Chapter 8: The Dynamics of Many Particle Systems
  • Chapter 9: Normal Modes and Waves
  • Chapter 10: Electrodynamics
  • Chapter 11: Numerical Integration and Monte Carlo Methods
  • Chapter 12: Percolation and Clusters
  • Chapter 13: Fractals and Kinetic Growth Processes
  • Chapter 14: Complex Systems
  • Chapter 15: Monte Carlo Simulations of Thermal Systems
  • Chapter 16: Quantum Systems
  • Chapter 17: Visualization and Rigid Body Dynamics
  • Chapter 18: Seeing in Special and General Relativity
  • Chapter 19: Epilogue -- The Unity of Physics

Additional examples, source code, and errata are available from the OSP website:

http://www.opensourcephysics.org.
